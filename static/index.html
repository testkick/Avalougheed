The Photoshoot

Three places I imagined you wearing them.

When I design, I picture the life a shoe will actually live and these felt right on a staircase in Positano, a lantern-lit alley in Kyoto, or a festival lawn at golden hour.

Materials & Craft

Nothing about this was rushed and I mean that literally.

I chose lamb hide for the inside and cow for the outside because it's how the ateliers I trained in still do it. I cut the upper myself for the first samples, stitched the rainbow on rather than glued it, and kept sending the sole back until it would soften with wear instead of just wearing out.

I'm only making a small batch so I found a family owned small generational factory that would take the same care as I did. I want it to last you a decade of walks on the streets of London, Paris, Rome, NYC, LA, Vancouver or wherever your heart takes.

The Designer

Ava Lougheed

Shoe Designer — trained at the Arsutoria School, Milan, Italy

I learned to build a shoe from the sole up at Milan's Arsutoria School, hunched over the same lasting benches designers have used for generations. I learned pattern-cutting by hand. It rewired how I see a shoe where every curve has to earn its place.

But most of my best ideas didn't come from the studio. They came from the walks I took to clear my head afterward. I would sketch soles on the side of the Duomo , deciding a shoe wasn't done until I trusted it on those cobble stone paths, not just a runway.

This is that shoe. The first one I will release. I hope it goes as many places for you as it has for me.
